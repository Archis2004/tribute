Oskar Schindler

A saviour in the Holocaust

Oskar Schindler with children in Tel-Aviv

Here's a time line of Oskar Schindler's life:

  • 1908 - Oskar Schindler is born in Zwittan, Moravia, Austria-Hungary to Sudeten German parents Johann and Franziska.
  • 1924 - Schindler is expelled from technical school for forging his report card. He does eventually complete his education, but chooses not to attend college.
  • 1928 - Schindler marries Emilie Pelzl, the daughter of a prosperous German farmer from Maletein.
  • 1935 - Schindler joins the separatist Sudeten German party and becomes a spy for the Abwehr, the intelligence service of Nazi Germany in 1936.
  • July 1938 - Schindler is arrested by the Czech government for espionage and imprisoned. He is released shortly afterwards under the terms of the Munich Agreement.
  • November 1938 - Schindler formally joins the Nazi Party.
  • January 1939 - Schindler is promoted to second-in-command of his Abwehr unit and relocates with his wife to Ostrava on the Czech-Polish border.
  • October 1939 - Schindler arrives in Krakow in Abwehr business. Emilie stays in Ostrava.
  • November 1939 - Schindler leases Rekord Ltd, an enamelware factory originally owned by several Jewish businessmen who were forced to sell after filing bankruptcy. Schindler renames the factory Deutsche Emaillewaren-Fabrik (German Enamelware Factory), also known as DEF.
  • 1943 - Schindler travels to Zionist leaders in Budapest on several occasions to testify about the Nazi mistreatment of the Jews.
  • 1944 - Schindler compiles his famous list of 1200 Jews that he then protects from the hands of the Nazis. By bribing Nazi officials, Schindler garners the release of any Jews on his list if they are caught. None of the Jews on the list are sent to concentrations camps or otherwise killed.
  • 1945 - After the war, Schindler is tried for war crimes due to his position as a member of the Nazi party. He is released after several Jews testify on his behalf.
  • 1949 - Schindler immigrates to Argentina, where he tries his hand at various businesses, all of which result in bankruptcy. He returns to Germany. All of his German business ventures also result in bankruptcy.
  • 1974 - Schindler dies and is buried in Jerusalem on Mount Zion. He is the only member of the Nazi party to be honored in this way.

"I hated the brutality, the sadism, and the insanity of Nazism. I just couldn't stand by and see people destroyed. I did what I could, what I had to do, what my conscience told me I must do. That's all there is to it. Really, nothing more."

--Oskar Schindler
